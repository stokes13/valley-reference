    Your Guide to Greater Phoenix & Maricopa County

    Phoenix

    Founded: 1867 | Population: 1,600,000

    America’s fifth-most-populated city is home to real cowboys, red-rock buttes and the kind of cactus most people see only in cartoons. It is the heart of the Sonoran Desert the gateway to the Grand Canyon, and its history is a testament to the spirit of ranchers, miners and visionaries.

    Tempe

    Founded: 1892 | Population:182,000

    Tempe, AZ one of America’s 100 Best Places to Live, has it all. A next-door neighbor of Phoenix, Tempe has its own special identity: progressive, a bit offbeat, and known for its rich arts and culinary scenes.

    Scottsdale

    Founded: 1951 | Population: 246,000

    Located in the beautiful Sonoran Desert, Scottsdale, Arizona is bordered by Phoenix to the west and the McDowell Mountains on the east.
    Scottsdale’s vibrant Old Town is considered the finest urban center in Arizona. It is home to more than 90 restaurants, 320 retail shops and more than 80 art galleries.

    Glendale

    Founded: 1891 | Population: 246,000

    Glendale is the sports destination for all of Arizona, as the NHL Coyotes continue to attract fans to new growth in the far western areas of the city. The state-of-the-art University of Phoenix Stadium in Glendale, Ariz. is also host to the Tostitos Fiesta Bowl and BCS Championship games as well as the 2008 Super Bowl.
